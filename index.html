The gap

Two sides of the same market failure

The problem is not a shortage of consultants with decks. It is a shortage of practitioners with lived experience, and any way to reach them before the decision gets made.

The affordability gap

Decisions made blind

Big 4 engagements for GCC setup, managed services design, or BCP run ₹80–200 lakh over three to six months. Mid-market enterprises, PE-backed companies, and new captives are locked out, left to attempt the mandate internally, hire juniors who have never run one, or lean on informal referrals with no governance.

So the same mistakes get made at scale, and re-learned across the industry at enormous cost.

The practitioner access gap

Judgment with nowhere to go

The people who have actually run GCCs, operationalised BOT transitions, deployed AI inside regulated BFSI back offices, or implemented ISO under regulatory scrutiny are working senior professionals. Their expertise is not organised, curated, or commercially available in any structured form.

LinkedIn exists. Referrals exist. A governed platform did not, until now.
